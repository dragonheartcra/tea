Benefits of Drinking Tea
 
Drinking tea has several benefits. It can refresh and energize, as theine and tannins stimulate the central nervous system, promote metabolism, and alleviate fatigue. Additionally, tea helps with weight loss and skin hydration by regulating fat metabolism and lowering blood fat and cholesterol. As a result, a variety of tea beverages are increasingly available on the market.
 
   
Tea Garden Planning   Tea Quality Identification   Brewing Process
 
The tea garden should be in areas with good transportation, ample water, and fertile soil. It should focus on tea cultivation and ecological management, with distinct zones for production, office, living, commercial, and cultural activities.   Tea quality refers to its appearance and internal characteristics. Appearance includes shape, color, and uniformity, while internal quality encompasses aroma, liquor color, taste, and the appearance of the brewed leaves.   First, warm the cup with water at 75-80°C. Add 3-5g of tea leaves to the cup. Next, rinse the tea leaves quickly to remove any tea fuzz. Finally, discard the rinse water and pour warm water along the sides of the cup to steep the tea for a few minutes.
